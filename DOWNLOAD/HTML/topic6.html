PRUSA SLICER: 3D PRINTING SOFTWARE

Prusa Slicer was the main software that would be used to design and actualy print 3D objects. We were taught how to configure the settings according to the 3D printer at Harvard, however those did not concern me a lot since I was not planning on getting my parts printed, or rather I couldn't. However, it was exciting to look at the method.

Ted Sirota's Presentation

This was a very exciting and interesting presentation given by Digital Fabricator Ted Sirota. Although I did not understand a lot of technicalities, it was interesting to see the kind of work possible in the field. The debate on which method would be better - having a HUGE 3D Printer or having lots of smaller ones and robot arms assembling them was absoultely riveting. I'm still rooting for the latter.

Readings & Documentation

This week we had to read Sparkfun's Introduction to Capacitors. A capacitor is a two-terminal, electrical component. Along with resistors and inductors, they are one of the most fundamental passive components we use. This tutorial explained everything from scratch - how they are made, how they work, units of capacitance, the types of capacitors, etc, etc.It was an interesting read that made me realize that almost all circuits have capacitors.
