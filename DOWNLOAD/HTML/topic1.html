PROJECT 'Cleaning Bot'

This project is for the creation of a vaccum cleaning bot that is controlled through an app on your phone. A sensor would help it avoid collisions and estimate jumps such as in case of stairs. Responses would be voice-controlled. The bot would ideally be for two purposes - dry vaccum and mopping the floor.

The same bot could be used as a transportation instrument. The map or floor plan of the location can be fed into the system. With a command, the bot would just got to that location. It makes it easier, let's say to send food from the kitchen to the dining room.
After further consideration, I've decieded to narrow the scope of my project. The transportation part is off the table. I could maybe incorporate this outside of class when I get time. For now, I would focus on the main purpose- dry vaccum and wet mopping. I have rough designs ready for both of these seperately, each design being significantly different from the other but what I'm essentially trying to do now is incorporate both these functionalities into one model. As of now, the design I have in mind is battery operated anr is to be manually turned on with the help of a switch, but I plan on making it a chargaable model with bluetooth acess, if possible.

PROJECT 'KeepTab'

This project has varied applications and can be used from a busy college student to a middle aged worker to an old person suffering from memory problems. Project 'KeepTab' is like a personalized Assistant in the form of a wearable device. It could be made voice controlled or phone / gesture controlled and it would rely only on your phone and the wearable object. It will help you set alarms, reminders and much more. Its responses will be voice automated. With the help of a tracking device, it will also help keep track of every object you pick up - leave at a certain place. This will help you recall where you left your car keys or where you left your assignment papers. The modern day carelessness and busy lifestyle can be solved with the help of this device.
This project was really close to me, considering that the inspiration for this came from my busy schedule and my habit to procrastinate and forget things.
To make it more viable, I have narrowed down the scope to something very simple - reminders and alarms. This would be a Smart Alarm and I would not have to manually set any alarm, ever. It would be connected to my Google calander and a few other apps. All meeting timings mentioned would be automatically set as an alarm. I plan on adding more features to this along the way. I found this video on YouTube and it helped me get a rough idea of how I want this project to be.

PROJECT 'PLANTS SPEAK'

With our busy lives, we often tend to not pay attention to plants around is. This project is about creating adevice that measures the pH of the soil, water content and sensitivity to sunlight. It blares an alarm whenever something goes wrong and a notification pops up in all devices connected with reports and the exact modifications required to bring stability.

Our first assignment for the week was to read the first three chapters of Making Things Move. We were also supposed to take notes of some interesting points we learnt.

The first chapter was like a recap for everything I had learnt during my sophomore year except the part about the gears, whcih was something new and fascinating for me. On reading about the minimum constraints design, I understood how factors are considered before implementing any design. In particular, I was fascinated by Rube Goldberg's breakfast machine project.
Chapter 2 was more of an essential because it talks about a challenge every maker faces - picking the right materials and using them the right way. I think it was fascinating to read about how alloys have different naming schemes and how materials play such an important role in making different kinds of diving boards.
Chapter 3 was specifically very useful for me as I did not know the uses of scres, nuts and bolts to such a great extent. I think this knowledge will be imperative for me while compiling my final project. I would suggest all those who like to build things and are interested in engineering to read this book.
